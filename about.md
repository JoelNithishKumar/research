I am a pre-doctoral research candidate based in Chicago, working toward a PhD in empirical economics.

My research interests center on how households and firms make decisions under risk — particularly in the design of social insurance, climate adaptation, and policies that interact with limited information or behavioral frictions. I want to use administrative data, field experiments, and quasi-experimental methods (difference-in-differences, instrumental variables, regression discontinuity) to produce credible causal estimates that can inform real policy.

I completed an M.S. in Information Technology and Management (Data Analytics) at Illinois Institute of Technology in May 2025, and am currently applying for pre-doctoral research fellowships. I read Angrist and Pischke. I use Git for everything.
