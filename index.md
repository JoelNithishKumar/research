I work on the empirical economics of decision-making under risk: the design of social insurance, climate adaptation, and policies that interact with limited information or behavioral frictions. I am most interested in combining administrative data, field experiments, and quasi-experimental designs — differences-in-differences, instrumental variables, regression discontinuity — to produce credible causal estimates that inform real policy choices.

I completed my M.S. at Illinois Institute of Technology in May 2025 and am preparing to apply for economics PhD programs.
